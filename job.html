Ideal Job

Description

Skills and Qualifications

Being a software developer means that you are responsible for creating,
testing and updating programs and their respective software for clients. This
specific job calls for both front and back end development, which includes
programming the logic in the parts 'behind the scenes' of the program, while
also developing and designing the User Interface for the solution. This job is
appealing to me as it combines both ends of programming and design, while
also creating solutions for everyday problems. With such a scope, one can
design a well-developed experience for users on both sides of the 'screen'
per-sei. Having arms into both programming and design allows more creative
thinking and innovation, and thought processes alongside how a program
could run, which grants opportunities to adaptively problem solve, create and
innovate on the go, while interacting with clients, and fullfilling their needs.
I feel as if my personality and talents would suit well into this position in the
future, and thus, I see it as a great, ideal and opportunistic job that I can aim
for, post-graduation.

Skills that are recommended for this job revolve around both programming
and technological frameworks knowledge, a supportive, friendly, authentic
personality who is both open minded and curious, as well as being well
mannered in communication. I believe that I obtain most of these skills to
date, except knowledge in the specific languages and technolgical
processes listed, as well as personal trouble with outward communication. I
know, however, that I can work on these skills in the following years, during
my university education, to target learning programming and technological
foundations, as well as connecting with new people and building better social
skills. To get into this type of job, I require finishing my studies, and then
gaining at least a year experience in a development role, which could be done
through an internship post-graduation.

Plan

Currently, I don't have any qualifications apart from a completion of VCE, but possess skills of creativity, openness, and a love for technology. I am also a
supportive person, who always seeks for authenticity with relationships. However, I am not the best with communication, or have experience in the listed
technological/programming processes. Thus, I plan to finish my studies in the bachelor of IT, and then go for an internship/Entry-Level job where I can
train specifically in the areas of software development, while also perhaps studying a Masters. Then with the experience gained, I should be able to get a
similar position in the industry.
