Personality Profile

Myers-Briggs Personality Test:

According to this 16Personalities test, I am an 'Advocate' which outlines that I
am a very thoughtful, curious, imaginative, feeling-orientated, decisive person
who is also sensitive to build-up of emotion. To me, this clarifies that I am a
'thinking' kind of person, able to problem solve a issue/come up with ideas
and perhaps also give insight on how to do these, but have trouble creating the
solution itself. Thus, when looking for a group, I should aim to support people
who have a more action-orientated mindset, who can create from ideas for the
best efficiency. In a group, I'd more than most likely take the role of a problem
solver, who can come up with ideas to circumnavigate issues and think things
through. I would also contribute by testing and thinking of new ways to do
things and tasks in the project to help provide the best solution.

Big 5 Personality Test:

This test also agrees that I am a thinking, feeling-oriented, introverted person,
while also being 'agreeable' and creative with high intellect. This test also
states that I am careful and dilegent, which are both positive traits to have in
group-orientated work, confirming my previous allocation to being a creative
problem solver.

Learning Styles Test:

This test states that my learning style consists of mainly theoretical/reflective
learning, indicating that I solve problems logically and through organisation,
while also prefering to stand back and observe, collecting and analysing
information, thinking of the big picture. Next to these is the pragmatic
learning style, where learning and trying out new ideas and theories on the go
is a big focus, which only supports the position of a creative problem solver in
this case. And last is the Activist style, which indicates that I am less into
immersing myself in an experience, but more observe the results of such.
