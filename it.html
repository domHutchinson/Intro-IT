So... Why IT?

Technology has been always been quite a major part of my life, solving even the littlest of problems and innovating new ways of thinking
and connecting with people. From the original Ipad, I always wondered what limits (if there even were any) could be broken with IT, and
these days, advancing new technologies have far surpassed even our wildest imagination with the amount of connection and solutions it
generates. This curiosity towards technology I have had since I was a child, as I felt, and still feel to this day, that I understood computers
more than I did people, I found them more relatable and comprehendable, and thus I experimented with apps and games whenever I had a
chance. At the start of high school I got my first computer, a Mac, and always spent bits of time experimenting with apps and writing code
to keep my brain entertained. As I grew older, there was less time for playing around, but still carried a love for technology, and thus, I
persued many tech-based classes in VCE, where I learnt about many aspects of Data Analytics and Computing. I found that programming
in Computing was a enjoyment to me, so I saught out universities which could offer me the opportunity to learn how to properly program
in a business setting, and teach valuable skills for later on in a career/life itself. In regard of what I wish to pursue, I am still coming to
terms of exactly the field I want to follow, although I hope to go down a path where I may be able to help people who need technological
solutions and also be able to help tell stories through a technological medium, like through a game, to perhaps encourage a different way
of thinking.

Interest in IT

I chose to come to RMIT thanks to the flexibility and versatility that the IT course operates on, having very flexible elective options,
programming courses and more, with outlooks on a business side also. As well as being well recognised for it's technology courses, RMIT
was a excellent option for studying this particular bachelor, thanks to it's openness and flexibility, even in these strange semi-not-really-
normal COVID recovery times, which suited my schedule quite well.
In these studies, I wish to gain knowledge and experience in most of IT's fields, and see which one I'd like to specifically pursue, while
also diving into bigger IT concepts and how to apply them in a business for later
